# Turing Tag LinkedIn Launch Post - Draft v1

**Target:** Tuesday Take, 220-250 words
**Voice:** John Hunter (Thompson/Twain/Mencken)
**CTA:** Drive to turingtag.com + solicit feedback

---

Half the posts in your feed were written by a machine and you have no way of knowing which ones.

That's not a complaint. It's a design problem.

I built something called the Turing Tag -- a voluntary content authorship standard. Three badges, three letters, one honest question: how was this made?

A means AI wrote it. B means it was blended, human and AI working together. C means crafted, every word human. You pick your own badge. You attach it to your own work. Nobody verifies it and nobody enforces it.

That last part is the whole point. Every "AI detector" on the market is selling false certainty to anxious people. They don't work reliably and they never will, because the better AI gets at writing, the worse detection gets at catching it. It's a losing arms race dressed up as a product.

The Turing Tag goes the other direction. Instead of trying to catch liars, it gives honest people a way to say what they did. Voluntary disclosure, the same principle that makes creative commons licensing work, applied to the question Alan Turing asked seventy-five years ago: can you tell the difference?

I built an analyzer at turingtag.com where you can tag your own work or paste in content you've found and read the stylistic signals. It's v1, it's free, and I want to know what's broken.

Try it. Tell me what's wrong with it. Tell me what's missing.
